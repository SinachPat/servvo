# Servvo — Go-to-Market Playbook

**Strategy in one line:** channel-led (franchise consultants first), case-study-powered,
concentrated in one vertical so referrals compound inside a community.

**Sequencing:** nothing here scales until the pilot bar is met. Phase order:
consultants → conferences → content → communities. Paid acquisition is deliberately
absent until CAC math exists.

---

## 1. ICP recap

- **Primary:** US multi-unit restaurant operators, 3–50 locations, cloud POS
  (Toast/Square/Clover), owner/principal buyer ("Marcus").
- **Channel buyer:** franchise consultants & agencies advising 10+ brands ("Dana").
- **Disqualifiers (for now):** single units, legacy-POS-only estates, enterprise chains
  wanting custom paper before a case study exists.

## 2. Channel 1 — Consultant partner program

**Why first:** one warm intro surfaces a *cluster* of owners on the same systems; trust
is pre-built; CAC is a rev-share, not ad spend.

| Element | Design |
|---------|--------|
| Offer to consultants | 20% rev-share year one (or wholesale at 25% off for white-label under their brand) |
| Enablement kit | 1-page leave-behind · 90-second demo video · the case study · a co-branded "AI-readiness assessment" they run with clients |
| Ask | 2 qualified intros per quarter to stay in the program |
| White-label (post-GA) | Their logo on the dashboard shell; Servvo runs everything behind it |

## 3. Channel 2 — Conferences & expos

Target: regional restaurant shows and franchise expos where multi-unit owners actually
walk the floor (national shows only once a case study exists).

**The 90-second live demo** (the whole booth): a seeded demo brand with 12 locations;
ask the agent, out loud, "which location had the worst labor percentage last week, and
why?" — then show the audit log entry it produced. The audit log is the trust closer.
Capture: badge scan + "want this snapshot for your own stores?" → assessment tool.

## 4. Channel 3 — Content engine (plain-English, operator-first)

| Asset | Cadence | Purpose |
|-------|---------|---------|
| "What an AI connector actually is, for restaurant owners" explainer | Once, evergreen | The SEO/anchor piece — owns the non-technical MCP query |
| YouTube: real setup, unedited, timer on screen | Monthly | Proves the 30-minute claim |
| LinkedIn: one operator-pain post/week (from discovery quotes) | Weekly | Feeds the consultant channel |
| Case study | Per pilot cohort | The only asset that closes |
| Reddit AMA (r/restaurantowners, r/franchise; r/mcp for credibility) | Quarterly | Community trust; strictly no-pitch |

Voice rules per the brand package: plain-spoken, name real systems, never "leverage
synergies," never overpromise security.

## 5. Outbound templates

**Consultant intro (email):**
> Subject: AI your franchise clients can actually turn on
> [Name] — I run Servvo. We make the systems your clients already use (Toast, Square,
> 7shifts) readable by AI assistants — connected in 30 minutes, read-only by default,
> every action logged. [Pilot brand] cut their Monday reporting from 3 hours to one
> question. I'd like to give your clients pilot access and you first rights on the
> white-label. 15 minutes this week?

**Operator DM (LinkedIn, post-case-study):**
> Saw you run [X] locations on Toast. We just helped a [N]-unit group ask their AI
> "which store missed labor targets last week" and get a real answer — no developers,
> 30-minute setup, nothing can touch money without your say-so. Worth a look at how
> [pilot brand] uses it?

## 6. Battlecards

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We'll just use Zapier" | Zapier runs pre-drawn workflows; it can't answer an open question across your stores. Servvo makes your systems *conversational* — and it's read-only until you say otherwise. |
| "Toast is adding AI" | Toast's AI stops at Toast's walls. Your 4 Square stores are invisible to it. Servvo reads the whole estate — and you keep it if you ever switch POS. |
| "We'll have a developer build it" | That's $40K+, 3 months, and maintenance forever. Servvo is $299/mo, live this week, and we maintain the connectors when vendors change their APIs. |
| "Is this safe?" | Read-only by default; money actions are off until you enable them, capped, and always need a human yes; every agent action is in an audit log you can read. [Show the log.] |

## 7. Funnel & targets (first two GA quarters)

Assessment-tool completions → qualified demos (target 25%) → pilots/trials (target 40%)
→ paid (target 60%) · consultant-sourced deals ≥50% of closes · CAC payback <6 months
on Basic, <3 on Pro. Kill any channel that doubles those paybacks after one quarter.
